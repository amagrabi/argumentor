Overview

The platform is designed to help you sharpen your reasoning and decision-making skills.

  • Challenge Yourself: Tackle thought-provoking questions across diverse fields.
  • Construct Your Argument: Formulate clear claims and support them with coherent reasoning.
  • Receive Feedback: Get AI-driven evaluations with ratings, explanations, and an analysis of your argument structure.
  • Analyze and Improve: Analyze weaknesses and track your progress as you earn experience points (XP) and advance through levels.

Every step is designed to provide insights into your reasoning style, helping you evolve and master the art of argumentation.

Profile Page

In your user profile, you can find an overview of your progress. It shows your level, XP, a graph of your progression over time as well as a detailed list of all your submitted answers.

Detailed evaluation feedback for each of your submissions is available, giving you insights into your strengths as well as areas where you might improve. Use this information to identify recurring weaknesses and adjust your approach to crafting stronger arguments.

Challenge Feature

In addition to submitting your primary argument, you have the option to engage with a challenge. This challenge is designed to help you identify any logical inconsistencies, unclear points, or areas where your argument may be improved.

Responding to the challenge will prompt a secondary evaluation and may earn additional XP. The challenge feature is optional, you can also move on to another question.

How XP is Calculated

Your performance is rewarded with XP, which help you level up and track your progress. XP is only awarded when your claim and supporting argument meet a minimum relevance threshold ({{ relevance_threshold }}/10).

Once your response passes this relevance check, the XP is calculated based on six key dimensions: Relevance, Logical Structure, Clarity, Depth, Objectivity, and Creativity. The average score from these criteria (each rated on a scale from 1 to 10) is multiplied by 10 to determine the total XP earned.

For example, if your performance in those six dimensions averages out to 6 out of 10, you would receive 60 XP for that response.
